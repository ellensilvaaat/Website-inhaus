## **A relaxed, down-to-earth chat about your goals**

The consultation isn’t about impressing anyone with design terms or mood boards. It’s about clarity. Most people walk in with a few ideas, some reference images, and a general sense of what isn’t working in their current home. 

Inhaus Living’s team is skilled at translating that into a practical starting point. They’ll ask open questions about how you use the space now, what’s frustrating, and what you’d like to change. Even if you don’t have all the answers yet, that’s fine. The team helps you figure out what matters most and where to begin. 

## **Practical advice based on experience, not just trends**

Design trends come and go. Some are worth leaning into. Others might not hold up so well over time. The consultation is a good place to get an honest take on what will work in your home. 

If you’re planning **home renovations in Fyshwick**, local knowledge counts for a lot. The team understands the quirks of certain builds, what’s likely behind those old walls, and how to make new elements feel integrated. 

They’ll walk you through considerations that might not have been on your radar yet. Things like natural light direction, ceiling heights, structural limitations. It’s these details that often shape the design more than any Pinterest board can. 

## **Early discussions around budget and scope**

Talking about budget early helps keep the project grounded. Inhaus Living is upfront about what different kinds of home renovation builders can deliver at various price points. That kind of honesty builds trust. 

You’re not locked into anything during the consultation, but you’ll start to get a clearer picture of what’s feasible. Maybe it’s a staged approach over time. Maybe there’s a way to repurpose existing elements instead of ripping everything out. 

It’s not about pushing for the biggest build possible. It’s about finding a practical path to achieve what you want without the process spiralling.
